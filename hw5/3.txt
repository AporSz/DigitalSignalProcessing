3. What is an antialiasing filter? What is its role (benefits & limitations) ?

An antialiasing filter is a low pass filter that removes undesired high frequencies that would otherwise lead to aliasing.
This ensures the "peaks" of the Fourier transform don't overlap after sampling. The filter can remove information which can lead
to minimal anomalies. (For example the rectangle function is infinite and it's Fourier transform is the sinc function. Applying
an antialiasing filter on it would lead to function that isn't a perfect sinc function, because we're cutting off infinite
ripples of the sinc function.)

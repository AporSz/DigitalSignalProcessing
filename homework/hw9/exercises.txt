1. Explain the relationship between filters and linear time invariant systems.

A filter is fundamentally a physical or mathematical implementation of a Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) system.
By taking advantage of LTI properties, a filter predictably alters the amplitude and phase of specific frequencies in
an input signal. Because filters are LTI systems, their exact behavior can be completely characterized by their impulse
response in the time domain or their transfer function in the frequency domain.
